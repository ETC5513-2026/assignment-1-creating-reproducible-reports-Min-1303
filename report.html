Assignment 1: Marketing Campaign Analysis

Author

Min

Introduction

This report analyses data from a personalised marketing campaign conducted by an online retailer over the last quarter. The campaign varied the level of marketing spend across individual customers through advertisements, emails, and promotional offers. The purpose of this analysis is to investigate whether higher marketing spend per customer is associated with higher purchase amounts. Understanding this relationship helps evaluate whether increased marketing investment results in stronger sales outcomes.

Research Question

The research question explored in this report is whether customers who receive higher marketing spend tend to make larger purchases. The analysis focuses on examining the relationship between marketing spend and purchase amount using summary statistics and visualisations. This investigation aims to assess whether increased marketing investment is associated with higher customer spending.

Dataset Introduction

The dataset contains customer-level information from a personalised marketing campaign conducted by an online retailer. Each observation represents an individual customer and includes variables describing marketing activity and purchasing behaviour. Key variables include marketing_spend, which records the amount spent targeting each customer, and purchase_amount, which records the value of purchases made by the customer. Prior to analysis, the dataset was cleaned by standardising column names, removing duplicate observations, excluding the notes column, and filtering out invalid numeric values in key variables. These steps ensure the dataset is consistent and suitable for analysis.

Dataset Description

The cleaned dataset contains 132 observations and 6 variables. The dataset includes variables describing customer marketing exposure and purchasing behaviour. Important variables used in this analysis are marketing_spend, visits, and purchase_amount.

Results

The relationship between marketing spend and purchase amount is explored using visualisations. Figure 1 illustrates the association between marketing spend per customer and purchase amount. Figure 2 compares purchase amounts across groups of marketing spend levels. These figures help assess whether higher marketing investment is associated with increased customer purchasing behaviour.

The visualisations suggest a positive relationship between marketing spend and purchase amount. In Figure 1, the fitted trend line indicates that customers receiving higher marketing spend tend to generate higher purchase amounts. Figure 2 further shows that customers in higher marketing spend groups generally have larger purchase values compared with those in lower spend groups. This suggests that increased marketing investment may be associated with higher customer purchases, although this analysis does not establish causality.
